SEA Hackathon 2018

August 2018

It has been a while since I joined my first hackathon few years ago. With more knowledge that I have so far, I tried to build something that may be useful for company's employees well being. The topic for this year's SEA hackathon revolved around 'productivity' and 'mental health'. After discussing with my team, we decided to build a device that can signal when you are busy so that other people will not disturb you (unless it is reaaally urgent!) for a specific time e.g. 25 minutes. As a result, we managed to build a mini device that can detect whether you are on your desk and you can change your status to 'busy' with a click of a (hardware and virtual) button. It is using Raspberry Pi as its brain and some LEDs as indicators. We also created a web dashboard with Node.js + Vue.js where you can see other people's status and click the virtual button. Even though we did not win any prize, it was a fun and rewarding experience!

PPIS Aid to Geranting

PPI (Indonesian Student Union) Singapura organised social trip to Geranting island on 21st-22nd May 2016. I really love the people there: they do not hesitate to greet foreigners like us. In this trip, we taught the children how to make arts and craft with used plastic bottles. We hope that they can turn used plastic into something more useful in the future. Some of us painted the school wall on the second day while the rest were busy building foundation for water container. One of the main difficulties there is access to clean water. Sometimes the villagers have to travel to the neighbouring islands just to get drinkable water. It makes us understand how water that we take for granted in Singapore is scarce commodity in the island.

Batam Social Mission

Together with PINUS (Indonesian Student Union in NUS) Social division 2016/2017, we embarked on a social mission to help the orphanage in Vistos Kasih, Batam. In this one day trip, the volunteers conducted station games to kick start the event. While taking a break in between the games, they share their experience in Singapore to the orphans. I was in charge in the kitchen where we cook Balinese style ayam sambal bawang for lunch. Even though the trip was short but it was certainly a meaningful one.
